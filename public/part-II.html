About Communication

In this part, we again use the form of two opposing devices. Cycling through a selection of actions and reactions, the devices interpret and respond to one another solely via the camera and the screen. The sequential device states each embody a unique non-verbal gesture, attitude, or emotion â€” e.g. meeting, waiting, transacting, play, competition, aggression, boredom, mimicry, impatience.

Passing an object to the other, perhaps using the other to 'hold' the object, as in short-term memory, or to perform a process.

Here we talk about the different phases of the conversation: calibration, busy/waiting, decision-making and transaction.
